# ExtraHelper — design context

## Design Context

### Users

Restaurant staff, mid-service, under time pressure. Two contexts that matter **equally** — the interface must genuinely adapt to both, not shrink from one to the other:

- **Tableside**: waiters on phones/tablets, one-handed, standing, often in low light or glare. Tap targets and reachability dominate.
- **Counter**: cashiers on large screens with a keyboard and mouse. Density, scanning, and keyboard speed dominate.

The job: get an order in fast and correctly, then get back to the guest. Every second of hesitation is a second not spent on the floor. Secondary surfaces (settings, reports, team) are calmer, desk-bound admin work for owners and managers.

### Brand Personality

**Bold, high-contrast, legible-at-a-glance.** Confident rather than decorative — the boldness is in service of readability across a noisy kitchen or a sunlit terrace, not in service of looking impressive. Strong type, strong colour blocks, unambiguous state.

Emotional goal: **certainty**. Staff should never wonder whether the tap registered, which table they're on, or whether the order went through. Loud where it matters (state, totals, destructive actions), quiet everywhere else.

Voice: plain, direct, no jargon. Say "Bill requested", never `bill_requested`.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: light and dark both first-class (per-user preference already ships, alongside a text-size scale — respect both; never assume a viewport size or a base font size).
- **Type**: Figtree (`--font-sans`, also `--font-heading`). Size and weight carry hierarchy.
- **Palette**: shadcn `base-nova` / `mauve` tokens. Tinted neutrals, never pure black or white. Semantic colour is consistent app-wide and already established:
  - `emerald` = good / balanced / free
  - `amber` = warning / low / occupied / over
  - `destructive` = error / short / loss
  - `blue` = informational / reserved
  - `orange` = attention / bill requested
- **Icons**: lucide only.
- **Numbers**: `tabular-nums` on every figure that sits in a column. This is a money app.
- **Photos**: **photo-first in the POS grid** — staff recognise dishes by picture faster than by reading. Large images drive the grid; text supports. A menu that isn't fully photographed must still look deliberate, so placeholders are designed, not empty.
- **Anti-references**: no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no purple-on-dark "AI" palette, no decorative sparklines, no cards nested in cards.

### Design Principles

1. **Legible across the room.** If it can't be read at arm's length on a phone in a bright dining room, it's too small or too low-contrast. Bold type and strong contrast are the brand.
2. **Never colour alone.** Every state carries an icon, a label, or a sign as well as a hue. Kitchens are lit badly and staff are colourblind at the same rate as everyone else.
3. **Both hands and both contexts.** Touch targets ≥44px and full keyboard operation. Adapt the layout for phone vs counter; never amputate features on small screens.
4. **The tool states what it did.** Optimistic where it's safe, honest when it fails. Destructive actions (void, delete, remove) confirm first and name the real consequence.
5. **Region is configurable.** Currency, timezone, tax and fees come from tenant settings. Never hardcode a locale's assumptions into a surface.

### Accessibility bar

WCAG **AA** minimum: contrast, full keyboard operation, visible focus, and `prefers-reduced-motion` honoured. Motion is transform/opacity only, ease-out, 150–300ms.
